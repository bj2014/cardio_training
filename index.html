What Is Cardiovascular Training?

Commonly called "cardio," the goal is to challenge your heart, lungs, and blood vessels to become more efficient at delivering oxygen to your muscles and clearing out metabolic waste.

Increased Heart Rate

Targets >50% of your Maximum Heart Rate (MHR).

Sustained Duration

Typically requires a session length of 10+ minutes.

Energy Systems

Utilizes phosphagen, glycolytic, and oxidative pathways.

Rockport 1-Mile Test

Walk 1 mile as fast as possible. Use your time and heart rate in the formula:

VO2 Max = 132.853 - (0.0769 × Weight lbs) - (0.3877 × Age) + (6.315 if Male, 0 if Female) - (3.2649 × Time min) - (0.1565 × HR bpm)

The 80/20 Rule

To see the best results, avoid the "Gray Zone" (Zones 3 & 4). Spend 80% of your time in Zone 2 to build your aerobic base, and 20% in HIIT to raise your ceiling.

Zone 2 Protocol

Aim for 180-240 min/week. Intensity where you can talk but it's rhythmic. 45+ mins per session is ideal.

HIIT Protocol

Only 1-2 times/week. Try the Norwegian 4x4: 4 mins at >90% MHR, followed by 3 mins active rest.

Avoid the "Gray Zone"

Zone 5: Performance Peak
